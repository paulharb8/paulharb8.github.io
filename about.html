Projects & Interests

This page expands on a few areas I’m exploring in more depth. I like projects that help me learn by doing, especially if they involve telling a story with data or building something that others can use. When I work on web projects, I aim for clean structure, clear writing, and simple layouts that are easy to navigate. I try to write content that stands on its own, even for readers who are unfamiliar with the topic. For visuals, I focus on consistency in spacing, typography, and color, so the design supports the message instead of distracting from it.

I also enjoy small experiments—like testing different chart types, learning how CSS layout works in different scenarios, or creating short guides for classmates. These experiments often lead to useful patterns I can reuse: navigation bars that are easy to scan, card layouts that work well on mobile, and color palettes that balance contrast and personality. Over time, I want to grow this site into a collection of small, polished examples that reflect what I’m learning. If you have feedback, I’m always open to suggestions!

Things I’m focusing on right now

  • Improving writing and page structure for clarity
  • Trying out new color palettes and typography
  • Building small visualizations and interactive components
  • Creating reusable UI patterns (cards, nav, grids)
